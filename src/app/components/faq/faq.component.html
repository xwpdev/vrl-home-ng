Are the various types of equipment suitable for outdoor events?

Yes, some outdoor events can be hosted with our equipment so far the following are put into place:
  • A tent or shade for the equipment, regardless of the weather condition.
  • Flat surface area or ground floor
  • Adequate power supply

What is the required power for each rig?

Each rig requires one standard electrical outlet — 110V, 10 amps, 3-prong power outlet.

What is the required space for each rig?

360 VR:
  • Ideal: 5 feet by 5 feet (flat) area
  • Included In Space: 48-Inch round tall boy table and tallboy table chairs
Room Scale VR:
  • Ideal: 8 feet by 8 feet (flat) area with a 7+-foot-tall ceiling
  • Absolute Minimum: 5 feet x 6.5 feet
  • Included In Space: TV, TV stand, headset, and computer

Differences between 360 VR and Room-Scale VR?

According to Sample Description 360 VR, the 360 VR experience offers your guests a relaxed, seated VR experience. While wearing a VR headset, guests experience real-world video of actual locations from around the world, whether a trip to the bottom of the ocean or climbing Mount Everest. In addition to our real-world experiences, we offer access to 360 games and puzzles. Our room Scale VR offers a much more immersive VR experience than its counterpart 360 VR. In room-scale VR, guests are not only standing, but they have the ability to move around in an 8-foot by 8-foot space while interacting with virtual objects. Depending on your event and its location, we can help you decide which VR experience best suits your event.

How can VR activation help us generate leads at our events?

What set of people are not meant to use the VR?

Children who fall under the age of 13 are not to have access to our equipment. Watching videos or playing games in VR may affect the visual development of children. Moreso, when children of age 13 or younger use VR, adults are advised to limit and watch their usage time and ensure they take breaks on intervals.

Is there anyone who should not use VR?

A comfortable VR experience requires an unimpaired sense of motion and balance. It is not advised to use the headset when you are tired, sleepy, under the influence of drugs, have digestive problems, under emotional stress or anxiety, when suffering from cold, flu, headaches, migraines, or earaches, as this can make you prone to adverse symptoms. We usually recommend seeing a doctor before using the headset if you are pregnant, elderly, have pre-existing binocular vision abnormalities or psychiatric disorders, or suffer from a heart condition or other serious medical condition.
